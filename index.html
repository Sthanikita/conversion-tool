Traditional Land Measurment Unit of Nepal

In Nepal, traditional units of land measurement such as Ropani-Ana in hilly regions and Bigha-Katha in the Terai were historically used for taxation purposes, based on the land's productivity. Over time, the evolution of land surveying techniques progressed from Sarpat Napi (rope measurement) and Compass Napi to more advanced methods like plane table surveys with telescopic alidades. Today, the use of modern instruments like total stations has become common, reflecting technological advancements in land measurement. As a result, land measurement has become a highly sensitive issue, intertwined with ownership, taxation, and legal frameworks.

Ropani-Ana System of Measurement:

In the regime of Janga Bahadur Rana this system of land measurement was started in Nepal.The Ropani-Ana system of land Measurement is implemented in Valley areas of Nepal mostly in the hilly region of a country.

  • 1 Ropani= 74 feet x 74 feet = 5476 sq. feet = 508.74 sq. meters
  • 1 Ropani = 16 Anna = 5476 sq. feet = 508.74 sq. meters
  • 1 Anna = 4 Paisa = 342.25 sq. feet = 31.79 sq. meter
  • 1 Paisa = 4 Dam = 85.56 sq. feet = 7.95 sq. meter
  • 1 Dam = 21.39 sq. feet = 1.98 sq. meter

Bigha-Katha-Dhur Measurement System:

This land measurment system is mostly used in the terai region og Nepal.

  • 1 Bigha= 270 feet x 270 feet
  • 1 Bigha= 20 Kattha = 72900 sq. feet= 6772.63 sq. meter
  • 1 kattha= 20 Dhur= 3645 sq. feet= 338.63 sq. meter
  • 1 Dhur = 182.25 sq. feet=16.93 sq. meter
